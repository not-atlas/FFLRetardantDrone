# VTOL Firefighting UAV Preliminary Sizing Engine

A high-fidelity Python design framework for the conceptual sizing, energy analysis, and mass-convergence of hybrid fixed-wing VTOL quadplanes. 

The suite contains two major components:
1. `vtol_sizing.py`: The native physics engine, parameter-sweep calculator, and plotting routine.
2. `interactive_explorer.html`: A static Javascript web-dashboard to flexibly dissect multidimensional arrays generated by the physics engine natively in your browser.

## Code Architecture

The architecture relies strictly on isolated data structures preventing state-leakage during massive multi-dimensional parameter sweeps.

- **`Assumptions`**: A 60+ parameter dataclass strictly defining independent `Inputs`. These act as the boundary conditions encompassing Mission parameters, Aerodynamics, Rotor Constraints, and Hardware structural limits.
- **`SizingResult`**: A 60+ parameter dataclass strictly defining calculated `Outputs`. All variables within this structure are derivatives dependent entirely on constraint derivations from `Assumptions`.
- **`compute_aircraft`**: The master physics solver. It maps the dependencies listed below across a 50-cycle damped-relaxation loop to explicitly resolve structurally-sound Aircraft Designs until standard MTOW error falls beneath `0.5%`.
- **Sweep Logic**: Clean wrapper functions (`run_1d_sweep` / `run_2d_sweep`) clone the baseline configurations, inject parameter bounds, and yield `pd.DataFrame` matrices storing results.

## Calculation Process (Inputs -> Outputs)

The engine enforces a rigid sequential mathematics pipeline to extract aircraft viability constraints:

1. **Mission Velocities**: Calculates Response constraints (Outbound Distances vs Required Response Times).
2. **Wing Geometry (Aero Constraints)**: Wing area is sized defensively explicitly to bounded `Stall Speeds` (using $C_{L\_max}$) or bounded `Cruise Targets` (using $C_{L\_cr\_target}$), picking the larger physical constraint. Resultant geometries (Spans, Chords, MACs) and Kinematic limits (Reynolds Numbers) are bounded.
3. **Fixed-Wing Power Loads**: Calculates total Drag forces integrating parasite (wetted area & interference factors) against induced drag, resulting in Shaft and resultant Electrical Cruise Power demands.
4. **VTOL Power Loads**: Derives raw VTOL Hover Shaft Powers scaling explicit Actuator Disk Loading profiles against Induced Velocities. Adds exact mathematical penalties for Vertical Climb profiles and multi-second Transitions. Calculates rotor tip-speeds to prevent supersonic constraints.
5. **Energy Integration**: Calculates fractional capacities for 10 independently timed Mission Segments (Takeoff, Climb, Transition, Outbound, Loiter, Target Drop, Return, Retries/Landings) mapping Time * Power.
6. **Electrical Pack Sizing**: Calculates specific Peak Amperage and Voltage constraints checking boundaries against user-defined Pack Specific Energies (Wh/kg) yielding pure Physical Battery Mass limits.
7. **Mass Budget Convergence**: Finally, empirical system masses (Avionics, Payload Bays, Frame Densities bounds) stack onto battery properties to deduce total Empty Weights. The loop recursively updates `MTOW` guarantees until the aircraft mathematically proves it carries its own power.

## Constant Efficiency Factors
The following factors are typically kept constant or use identified defaults for standard evaluation:
- **Propulsive Efficiency ($\eta_{prop}$)**: `0.75` (Fixed-wing cruise propeller)
- **Hover Figure of Merit (FoM)**: `0.65` (Rotor hover efficiency)
- **Oswald Efficiency Factor ($e$)**: `0.70` (Induced drag correction)
- **Electrical Efficiency ($\eta_{elec}$)**: `0.90` (Motor and ESC combined)
- **Air Density ($\rho$)**: `1.225 kg/m³` (Sea level standard)

## Plot Detail & Equations

Below is the mathematical derivation and process utilized for each of the primary trade studies.

### A. Wing Sizing Plots
1. **Aspect Ratio vs Span**: Calculates the physical wingspan required for a given area.
   - Equation: $b = \sqrt{AR \cdot S}$
2. **Aspect Ratio vs Root Chord**: Derives the required root chord length for the planform.
   - Equation: $c_{root} = \frac{2S}{b(1 + \lambda)}$
3. **Aspect Ratio vs Tip Chord**: Calculates chord at the wing tip based on taper.
   - Equation: $c_{tip} = \lambda \cdot c_{root}$
4. **Aspect Ratio vs Mean Aerodynamic Chord (MAC)**: Computes the reference chord for aero calculations.
   - Equation: $c_{mac} = \frac{2}{3} c_{root} \frac{1 + \lambda + \lambda^2}{1 + \lambda}$
5. **Stall Speed Target vs Required Wing Area**: Determines minimum area for low-speed flight.
   - Equation: $S_{stall} = \frac{2W}{\rho V_{stall}^2 C_{L\_max}}$
6. **Total MTOW vs Required Wing Area**: Shows area scaling as the aircraft gets heavier.
   - Process: $S = \max(S_{stall}, S_{cruise})$ where $S_{cruise} = \frac{2W}{\rho V_{cruise}^2 C_{L\_cr\_target}}$
7. **Wing Area vs Actual Minimum Cruise CL**: Reveals how "over-winged" the plane is in cruise.
   - Equation: $C_{L\_cr} = \frac{W}{q S}$
8. **Aspect Ratio vs Induced Drag (CDi)**: Shows reduction in induced drag with AR.
   - Equation: $C_{Di} = \frac{C_L^2}{\pi e AR}$
9. **Aspect Ratio vs Total Drag (CD0)**: Tracks overall drag coefficient changes.
   - Equation: $C_{D\_total} = C_{D0\_total} + C_{Di}$
10. **Cruise Speed vs Cruise Drag Forces (N)**: Tracks actual aerodynamic resistance.
    - Equation: $D = q S C_{D\_total}$
11. **Cruise Speed vs Cruise Electrical Power (W)**: Required battery draw for level flight.
    - Equation: $P = \frac{D V}{\eta_{prop} \eta_{elec}} + P_{avionics}$
12. **Taper Ratios vs Chord Boundaries**: Evaluates planform shape limits using equations from 2 and 3.

### B. VTOL & Hover Kinetics
13. **Rotor Diameter vs Actuator Disk Area**: Total available thrusting area.
    - Equation: $A_{disk} = n \pi (\frac{D}{2})^2$
14. **Rotor Diameter vs Peak Hover Power**: Required power to sustain VTOL weight.
    - Equation: $P_{hov} = \frac{1}{\text{FoM} \cdot \eta_{elec}} \frac{(W \cdot T/W)^{1.5}}{\sqrt{2 \rho A_{disk}}}$
15. **Gross Total Mass vs Peak Hover Power**: Scaling of hover cost with aircraft weight.
    - Process: Uses equation from 14 with variable $W$.
16. **Rotor Diameter vs Electrical Peak Currents**: Amperage stress on the battery pack.
    - Equation: $I_{peak} = \frac{P_{peak}}{V_{batt}}$
17. **Rotor Diameter vs Single Takeoff Event Energy Draw**: Capacity drain for the hover phase.
    - Equation: $E_{hov} = P_{hov} \cdot t_{hov} / 3600$

### C. Mission Profiling & Batteries
18. **Cruise Speed vs Total Segment Energy Depletion**: Aggregated mission cost.
    - Process: $E_{total} = \sum (P_i \cdot t_i)$
19. **Outbound Operational Radius vs Total Energy Load**: Range sensitivity.
    - Equation: $E_{cruise\_out} = P_{cr} \cdot \frac{R}{V_{cr}}$
20. **Single Event Hover Time vs Total Energy Load**: Sensitivity to VTOL duration.
    - Process: $\frac{\partial E_{total}}{\partial t_{hov}} = P_{hov}$
21. **Battery Reserve Fraction vs Total Nominal Requirements**: Impact of safety margins.
    - Equation: $E_{nominal} = \frac{E_{used} (1 + k_{reserve})}{k_{usable}}$
22. **Usable Pack Factors vs Nominal Requirements**: Impact of depth-of-discharge.
    - Process: Uses equation from 21.
23. **Battery Specific Energy (Wh/kg) vs Required Battery Mass**: Impact of cell tech.
    - Equation: $M_{batt} = \frac{E_{nominal}}{e_{batt}}$
24. **Aircraft Total Assigned Mass vs Required Battery Mass**: Mass feedback loop.
    - Process: Iterative solution of $M_{total} = M_{empty} + M_{pay} + M_{batt}$.
25. **Required Cruise Bounds vs Battery Heaviness Penalty**: Speed/Weight trade-off (Coupled).
26. **Mission Analysis**: Stacked Graph of segment energies ($E_i$).
27. **Mission Analysis**: Discrete Step Power Burst Demands ($P_i$).
28. **Mission Timeline**: Instantaneous Power $P(t)$ across segments.
29. **Mission Timeline**: Remaining Energy $E(t) = E_{total} - \int P dt$.
30. **High Voltage Packs vs Output Load Peak Current**: Electrical stress vs Voltage choice.
    - Equation: $I = \frac{P}{V}$
31. **High Voltage Packs vs Absolute Capacity (Ah)**: Required charge storage.
    - Equation: $C_{Ah} = \frac{E}{V}$
32. **High Voltage Packs vs C-Rate**: Burst discharge stress.
    - Equation: $C_{rate} = \frac{I}{C_{Ah}}$

### D. Coupled Dependencies
33. **Aspect Ratios vs Mission Energy Integrations**: Aero efficiency impact on range.
34. **Aspect Ratios vs Required Drive Battery Masses**: Aero impact on system weight.
35. **Aspect Ratios vs Required Drive Battery Masses**: Aero impact on system weight.
36. **Contour Maps**: 2D trade of AR and Rotor Diameter on Battery Mass.
37. **Contour Maps**: 2D trade of Cruise Velocity and Rotor Diameter on Energy.
38. **Contour Maps**: MTOW and AR impact on Wing Span (Structural limit check).
